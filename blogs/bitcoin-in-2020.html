What will happen to Bitcoin in 2020?

There is no way of knowing for sure, but in the future, it could become as valuable as dollars or Euro and rise in value. But at the end of the day, the Bitcoin is only worth what people will pay for it. The great thing about Bitcoin is that if you have money in it, the bank will take the Bitcoin as payment for any transaction of value. So it's like a savings account. What are the current roadblocks to the rapid expansion of the Blockchain? Currently, the Bitcoin network is very overburdened by the increase in transactions. With that being said, I'm pretty confident we will be able to scale back the burden over the coming years. Today we have about five-and-a-half transactions per second, with a capacity of five-and-a-half transactions per second. What's the next step for the Blockchain? Going forward, our first product will be the AlphaProof Coin. When people buy the coin, they'll be charged a modest amount of BTC, and given a Proof-of-Asset to check the blockchain, and a Proof-of-Work for a public web service that will guarantee the exchange. And, of course, a clever part is that the Proof-of-Asset is at the network, and the PoW is hidden from the user, but at a central server. The public service will be up, but the PoW is very stealthy . It's an interesting centralization and I think it's worth looking into." How Bitcoin Core was created The creation of Bitcoin Core is quite interesting, as it has the unique distinction of being the first altcoin and is a fork of the original Bitcoin Core open source code. In other words, a step in the wrong direction. Yet, the fork is an interesting step forward in the modern economy.
