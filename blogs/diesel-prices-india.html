We are trying our best to ensure that no one in the country loses life due to shortage of drinking water, says Gujarat Governor Gujarat's drinking water availability, as per the year 2011-12, is 120 million litres. The 11 years to 2015-16 witnessed a 24 per cent increase in availability of drinking water in the state. According to data compiled by the Department of Drinking Water and Sanitation (DDWS) at Thane, as per availability data for the year 2011-12, the total availability of drinking water was 120 million litres. In 2015-16, the availability is estimated to be around 270 million litres. According to Gujarat government officials, the reasons for the jump n availability of drinking water are increased rainfall, desilting work done on rivers and canals as well as the construction of reservoirs in high plains. The drought situation has now become normal, according to Gujarat agriculture department officials, who said that the new trend indicates that rainfall will improve further. However, the reason for the increase in the demand is the weather pattern of coming rains. According to Chief Technical Advisor and Chief Scientist of the state's meteorological department P M Karthik, since last few days the annual average rainfall in the western half of the state has increased with strong thunderstorms making their appearance from November 24, 2014 onwards, bringing heavy downpour and flood into the adjoining states.
